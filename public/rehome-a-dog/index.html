Rehome a Dog Safely in South Africa

Private, respectful, and judgement-free guidance. Dog Rescue SA does not charge to use this directory.

Life changes. Moving house, emigration, financial pressure, a new baby, a change in health — sometimes rehoming your dog is the kindest choice. Whatever your reason, you deserve support that is private, respectful, and free of judgement. Dog Rescue SA does not collect your details or publish listings. When you follow a partner link, that rescue manages its own enquiry and privacy process.

Before you contact a rescue

Having a few things ready helps the rescue understand your dog's situation quickly:

  • A few recent photos of your dog.
  • Vaccination or vet records, if you have them.
  • An honest description of your dog's temperament, health, and behaviour — including anything that hasn't gone well.
  • Your rough timeline — whether this is urgent or you have some flexibility.

This is general preparation guidance. Each rescue will confirm exactly what it needs once you make contact.

What happens when you reach out

  1. You contact the right rescue for your dog's breed and province.
  2. The rescue may ask about your dog — including age, health, temperament, and your circumstances — so it can assess what help may be possible.
  3. Ask what happens while the rescue reviews the enquiry. Foster space, owner-care arrangements, screening steps, and timelines differ between organisations and cases.
  4. The rescue will explain its process. Capacity, assistance, placement, and timing cannot be guaranteed by Dog Rescue SA.

The process varies by rescue and situation. The important thing is that your dog goes to a safe, loving home — not to whoever answers a Facebook post first.

Frequently asked questions

Does it cost anything to rehome a dog through Dog Rescue SA?

Dog Rescue SA does not charge to use this directory. Partner rescues set their own policies, possible fees, and donation arrangements, so confirm these directly before proceeding.

Can I stay in touch with my dog after rehoming?

This depends on the rescue and the adopter. Some adopters are happy to share occasional updates. The rescue can advise on what's typical.

What if my dog has health or behaviour issues?

Be honest about health and behaviour. The rescue needs accurate information to assess safety and what help may be possible. Capacity and placement are never guaranteed, but being upfront supports a fair assessment.
